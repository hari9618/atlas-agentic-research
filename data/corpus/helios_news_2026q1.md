AUSTIN — Helios Robotics said on March 4, 2026 that it had signed the largest
contract in its history: a five-year, $900 million agreement to deploy more than
12,000 autonomous robots across the fulfillment network of a major North American
retailer. Shares of Helios rose 14% on the news.

Analysts called the deal a validation of the company's Fleet Software strategy, noting
that roughly 40% of the contract value is recurring software and support revenue. "This
locks in software ARR for years and deepens the moat," one analyst wrote.

The announcement also renewed scrutiny of Helios's supply chain. The company has
repeatedly flagged its dependence on a single Taiwanese actuator supplier, and the new
contract sharply increases the volume of robots it must ship in 2026 and 2027. Helios
executives said on a call that second-source qualification remains on track for late
2026 but acknowledged that the ramp leaves "limited margin for error" if the primary
supplier stumbles.

Helios's chief rival, Aster Dynamics, announced its own European expansion the same
week, underscoring how competitive the warehouse-automation market has become.
